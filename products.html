The Rosaceae family is said to be as ancient 30 million years old; the Middle East and in particular Iran being the place of its humble origin. Although it’s medicinal properties are numerous and profound, it’s famous more so for its rich aroma and distinctive, luxurious fragrance that has been used in perfumes for thousands of years. The plant it’s self has been harnessed in various form; the petals may be eaten whole or brewed in tea, whereas the hips or seed pods are more commonly used in extracts by western nutraceutical companies for oral consumption or as ointments and lotions.

Much research has been conducted into the health benefits this plant may have to offer. The rose contains many active ingredients including various Terpenes, Flavonoids, glycosides and fatty acids, owing to it’s large therapeutic window for various ailments. Much evidence has arisen suggesting possible neuroprotective affects, anti-convulsive, analgesic and anti-diabetic properties, amongst much else. Although clinical testing has demonstrated specific constituents of Rose extract may be affective for these ailments; we do not recommend nor claim our product treats these issues. Furthermore, studies have proven that many of the constituents found in the rose extract, notably the Flavonoids, in fact stimulate the GABAa receptors in the brain; perhaps explaining the calming affect experienced when one inhales the precious essential oils
